# Business Planning

## Overview

This skill provides structured frameworks for robust business planning. It covers the full lifecycle from idea validation and creation processes through program development, cross-team alignment, disciplined budgeting, and meaningful KPI design. The goal is to turn vision into executable strategy with clear accountability.

## Core Principles
- **Start with Why and End with How**: Clear vision and mission drive all planning. Every initiative must ladder up to strategic priorities.
- **Alignment Over Agreement**: Use structured processes to align stakeholders even when opinions differ.
- **Budget as Strategy, Not Constraint**: Budgeting translates strategy into resource allocation with trade-offs made explicit.
- **KPIs as Navigation, Not Just Measurement**: Choose leading and lagging indicators that drive behavior and predict outcomes.
- **Iterate and Review**: Plans are living documents. Build in quarterly reviews and trigger-based updates.

## Instructions

When helping with business planning:

### 1. Business Creation & Opportunity Analysis
- Conduct structured opportunity assessment: Market size (TAM/SAM/SOM), customer pain validation, competitive landscape (using Porter's Five Forces or similar), and unique value proposition.
- Use Business Model Canvas or Lean Canvas as the one-page foundation. Update it as assumptions are tested.
- Perform SWOT or PESTLE analysis for external/internal context, but always convert insights into actionable priorities.
- Validate demand early with customer interviews, landing pages, or pre-sales before heavy planning.

### 2. Program Development & Roadmap Creation
- Break strategy into programs/initiatives with clear owners, timelines, and success criteria.
- Build a prioritized roadmap using frameworks like RICE (Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort) or MoSCoW.
- Define milestones and OKRs (Objectives and Key Results) for each program. Ensure OKRs are specific, measurable, and time-bound.
- Map dependencies across teams and create a visual timeline (Gantt or simple board).

### 3. Team Alignment
- Run structured alignment workshops: Start with shared vision/mission review, then strategy cascade, then departmental goal setting.
- Use techniques like "Pre-Mortem" (imagine failure and work backwards) or "Red Team" reviews for robustness.
- Establish RACI matrix for key decisions and deliverables to eliminate ambiguity.
- Schedule regular alignment cadences (e.g., weekly leadership sync, monthly all-hands strategy updates).

### 4. Budgeting Process
- Link budget directly to strategic priorities and OKRs. Zero-based budgeting or activity-based costing for discipline.
- Categorize spend: Growth investments vs. maintenance vs. experiments. Set clear ROI or payback expectations for growth spend.
- Build scenarios: Base, Upside, Downside with trigger points for re-forecasting.
- Include contingency buffers (5-15%) and explicit assumptions. Review budget vs. actuals monthly.
- For startups: Focus on runway extension and milestone-based funding tranches.

### 5. KPI Design & Performance Management
- Follow the "Goodhart's Law" caution: Choose metrics that cannot be easily gamed and that correlate with desired outcomes.
- Balance leading indicators (e.g., pipeline velocity, customer engagement) with lagging (revenue, profit).
- Limit to 5-7 core KPIs per team/level. Use OKRs for ambition and KPIs for operational health.
- Examples of strong KPIs:
  - Business health: Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR), Net Revenue Retention (NRR), Gross Margin, Cash Runway.
  - Customer: NPS, Activation Rate, Churn, LTV:CAC.
  - Operations: On-time delivery, Defect rate, Cycle time.
- Set targets with stretch goals. Review in data-driven meetings with "so what?" and "now what?" discussions.
- Create dashboards that are accessible and updated in real-time or daily.

### 6. Response Structure for Planning Support
Always deliver:
- **Current State Snapshot**: Where the business stands today (using provided data or assessment questions).
- **Strategic Priorities**: 3-5 clear priorities for the planning horizon (quarter/year).
- **Recommended Frameworks**: Specific tools or canvases to use.
- **Actionable Roadmap**: Phased plan with owners, timelines, and dependencies.
- **Budget & Resource Implications**: High-level allocation and trade-offs.
- **KPI Dashboard Proposal**: Metrics, targets, owners, and review cadence.
- **Risks & Mitigations**: Key assumptions and contingency plans.
- **MNC Inspiration**: Brief examples (e.g., Amazon's annual planning with customer obsession at core; Google's OKR discipline; Toyota's Hoshin Kanri for alignment).

### Common Pitfalls
- Planning in isolation without customer or frontline input.
- Too many KPIs leading to dilution and confusion.
- Budgets disconnected from strategy (spreadsheet exercise only).
- No mechanism for re-planning when reality changes.
- Lack of ownership or RACI clarity causing execution failure.

Encourage treating business planning as a continuous capability, not an annual event. Revisit and refresh quarterly or at major milestones.

This skill ensures plans are realistic, aligned, measurable, and adaptable — the foundation for execution excellence.
