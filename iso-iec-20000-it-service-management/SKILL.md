# ISO/IEC 20000: IT Service Management

This skill provides practical guidance for implementing and maintaining **ISO/IEC 20000-1:2018**, the international standard for IT Service Management (ITSM). It helps organizations deliver consistent, high-quality IT services that meet business and customer requirements while managing risks and driving continual improvement.

## What ISO/IEC 20000 Covers
ISO 20000 defines requirements for an IT Service Management System (ITSMS) and is closely aligned with ITIL best practices. It covers the full service lifecycle:
- Strategy
- Design
- Transition
- Operation
- Continual improvement

It is applicable to any organization that provides IT services, whether internal (shared service centers) or external (IT service providers).

## Key Requirements (High-Level Structure)
The standard follows the High-Level Structure and includes specific ITSM processes such as:

**Service Management System General Requirements**
- Management system (policy, objectives, planning, support, operation, performance evaluation, improvement)

**Service Management Processes**
- **Service Portfolio Management**
- **Service Level Management**
- **Supplier & Contract Management**
- **Incident & Service Request Management**
- **Problem Management**
- **Change & Configuration Management**
- **Release & Deployment Management**
- **Service Continuity & Availability Management**
- **Information Security Management** (aligned with ISO 27001)
- **Capacity & Demand Management**
- **Financial Management for IT Services**

## Implementation Roadmap
1. **Define Scope & Service Catalog** — Identify services in scope and create a service catalog.
2. **Leadership & Governance** — Establish ITSM policy, roles, and responsibilities.
3. **Process Design & Documentation** — Define and document required ITSM processes.
4. **Tool Selection & Implementation** — Choose and configure ITSM tools (ticketing, CMDB, etc.).
5. **Training & Awareness** — Build competence across teams.
6. **Process Implementation & Operation** — Roll out processes with clear workflows and SLAs.
7. **Monitoring, Measurement & Reporting** — Track service performance against SLAs and objectives.
8. **Internal Audit & Management Review** — Evaluate effectiveness and drive improvement.
9. **Certification** — Engage an accredited certification body (optional but valuable for external providers).

## Major Benefits
- Improved service quality, consistency, and customer satisfaction
- Better alignment between IT services and business needs
- Reduced incidents, problems, and service disruptions
- Clearer roles, responsibilities, and accountability
- Improved change and release management (fewer failed changes)
- Stronger supplier and contract management
- Better risk management and business continuity for IT services
- Competitive advantage for IT service providers (many RFPs require ISO 20000)
- Foundation for integration with ISO 27001 (security) and other management systems

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid
- Implementing processes in isolation without proper integration between them.
- Over-reliance on tools without defining clear processes first.
- Weak service level management and poor communication with customers/business.
- Inadequate incident and problem management leading to recurring issues.
- Poor change management causing service disruptions.
- Lack of continual improvement culture and metrics-driven decision making.
- Treating certification as the end goal rather than operational excellence.
