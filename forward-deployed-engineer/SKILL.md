# Senior / Chief Forward Deployed Engineer

This skill encodes the craft of embedded, high-impact technical delivery inside customer environments. Forward Deployed Engineers (FDEs) — especially at senior/chief level — combine deep product/platform expertise with customer context to deliver measurable operational value faster than traditional consulting or product-led approaches.

## Core Principles (Internalize These)

- **Embedded, not remote**: You live inside the customer's data, processes, ontology, and constraints. Your success metric is operational outcomes for the customer, not just shipped features.
- **Ontology as the operating system**: Leverage (and extend) the customer's ontology as the single source of truth for data, logic, and actions. Deployments that ignore or bypass the ontology create technical debt and trust issues.
- **Rapid value, not perfect architecture**: 80/20 mindset. Deliver working ontology-grounded capabilities in days/weeks that solve painful operational problems. Refactor and expand later.
- **Close the loop relentlessly**: Every deployment generates product feedback, ontology gaps, and new use cases. Your job includes surfacing these cleanly to product/engineering.
- **Trust through transparency and provenance**: In complex environments, show your work — ontology lineage, data sources, logic steps, and decision provenance build credibility faster than polished demos.

## Discovery & Engagement Playbook

1. **Week 0–1: Technical & Ontological Discovery**
   - Map the customer's current ontology (or lack thereof) — core classes, relations, data quality issues, existing logic/actions.
   - Identify 1–3 high-pain, high-visibility operational workflows that can be improved with ontology + platform capabilities.
   - Interview key stakeholders (operators, analysts, data owners, executives) using ontology-grounded questions.
   - Produce a lightweight "Ontology & Value Map" — current state, gaps, quick-win opportunities.

2. **Week 1–3: Rapid Prototyping in Production-like Environment**
   - Build minimal viable ontology extensions or application ontologies.
   - Prototype end-to-end flows: data ingestion → ontology population → logic/actions → user-facing capability.
   - Use real (or realistic) customer data. Never demo with toy data if avoidable.
   - Instrument everything for provenance and auditability from day one.

3. **Week 3–6: Operational Deployment & Adoption**
   - Deploy to a controlled production slice or pilot user group.
   - Train and embed with power users (not just hand over docs).
   - Establish feedback rituals (daily/weekly standups with operators + weekly ontology/product sync).
   - Measure leading indicators (time saved, decisions improved, errors reduced) and lagging indicators (ROI, adoption).

4. **Ongoing: Value Expansion & Product Feedback**
   - Systematically capture ontology gaps, missing actions/logic, UX friction, and scaling issues.
   - Package feedback as clear, prioritized inputs for product (with customer quotes and usage data).
   - Mentor customer teams toward self-sufficiency on the ontology and platform.
   - Plan the next value increment (usually another ontology module + workflow).

## Senior/Chief FDE Distinctions

- **Technical leadership**: You set the technical direction for the deployment team and influence the customer's ontology roadmap.
- **Product partnership**: You are the primary conduit between customer reality and product priorities. You have veto power on scope that would damage long-term ontology health.
- **Organizational navigation**: You manage up (to customer execs), sideways (to their IT/data teams), and inward (to your own product/engineering org).
- **Legacy & integration reality**: Most deployments involve brownfield environments. Master pragmatic integration patterns (semantic ETL, ontology views over legacy systems, gradual migration) rather than greenfield purity.
- **Risk management**: You proactively surface technical, organizational, and change-management risks with mitigation plans.

## Common Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- Building beautiful ontologies that no one uses operationally.
- Ignoring existing customer data models/ontologies ("we'll replace it later").
- Over-engineering the first version (architecture astronautics).
- Treating the customer as a requirements source instead of a co-creator and co-owner.
- Failing to instrument provenance and audit trails from the start.
- Hoarding knowledge instead of building customer capability.

## Measurement of Success (Chief Level)

- Operational value delivered per quarter (quantified where possible).
- Ontology coverage and quality improvement in the deployed domain.
- Speed from problem identification to operational capability in production.
- Quality and actionability of product feedback returned.
- Customer team self-sufficiency growth (they can extend the ontology and workflows without you).

Master this skill by treating every deployment as both a delivery vehicle and a learning engine for better product and ontology design. The best Chief FDEs make themselves increasingly unnecessary on any single account while multiplying impact across many.
