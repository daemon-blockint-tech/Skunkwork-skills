# Cash Flow Management

## Overview

Many entrepreneurs fail not from lack of revenue or product-market fit, but from running out of cash. This skill teaches practical cash flow management to maintain liquidity, make smart asset purchase decisions, and deploy investment funds wisely. It emphasizes forecasting, discipline, and proactive decision-making over reactive firefighting.

## Core Principles
- **Cash is Oxygen**: Profit is an opinion; cash is fact. Prioritize cash preservation and generation above all else in early stages.
- **Forecast Religiously**: Build rolling cash flow forecasts and update them frequently with actuals.
- **Distinguish Timing from Amount**: Revenue and expenses rarely sync. Manage the timing gap aggressively.
- **Invest for Return, Not Prestige**: Asset purchases and fund deployment must have clear payback or strategic ROI.
- **Build Buffers and Options**: Maintain reserves and access to capital before you desperately need it.

## Instructions

When assisting with cash flow issues:

### 1. Cash Flow Forecasting & Monitoring
- Build a 13-week (or 12-month rolling) cash flow forecast: Start with opening cash balance, add all inflows (collections, investments, loans), subtract all outflows (payroll, vendors, debt service, capex).
- Use the direct method for short-term accuracy. Categorize by operating, investing, and financing activities.
- Update weekly with actual bank data. Track variance between forecast and actual (aim for <10-15% variance).
- Key metrics to monitor: Cash runway (months of cash left at current burn), burn rate (monthly net cash outflow), cash conversion cycle.
- Create scenarios: Base, Conservative (delayed collections), Aggressive (faster growth/higher spend).

### 2. Managing Inflows & Collections
- Accelerate receivables: Offer early payment discounts, tighten credit terms, automate invoicing and follow-ups, require deposits or milestone payments for projects.
- Diversify customer base to reduce concentration risk.
- For subscription businesses: Focus on reducing involuntary churn and dunning processes.
- Track Days Sales Outstanding (DSO) and set improvement targets.

### 3. Managing Outflows & Payables
- Extend payables strategically without damaging supplier relationships (negotiate better terms, use early payment discounts only when beneficial).
- Prioritize payments: Payroll and taxes first (legal and morale), then critical vendors, then others.
- Cut or defer non-essential spend ruthlessly. Implement zero-based review for recurring expenses.
- Control inventory tightly — excess inventory ties up cash (see Working Capital skill for deeper tactics).

### 4. Asset Purchase & Capex Decisions
- Apply strict hurdle rates: Require payback period < 12-24 months or IRR above cost of capital for most purchases.
- Use leasing or financing options instead of outright purchase when it preserves cash and flexibility.
- Distinguish between growth capex (investments that generate future cash) and maintenance capex.
- Before buying: Ask "What is the cash impact this month/quarter?" and "What revenue or cost savings does this unlock, and by when?"
- Example from MNCs: Amazon invests heavily in capex (warehouses, tech) but only when it clearly extends the flywheel and long-term cash generation.

### 5. Handling Investment Funds & Capital Raises
- Raise capital based on milestones and runway, not desperation. Target 18-24+ months runway post-raise.
- Deploy funds according to the plan presented to investors. Track "use of proceeds" transparently.
- Separate operating cash from investment/raise proceeds. Do not burn raise money on operating losses without clear path to efficiency.
- Maintain investor updates with cash position, burn, and runway — builds trust for future rounds.
- For bootstrapped or profitable businesses: Reinvest profits strategically while maintaining a cash reserve (e.g., 3-6 months operating expenses).

### 6. Crisis Cash Management
- When runway < 6 months: Immediate actions include salary deferrals/furloughs (with transparency), vendor negotiations, asset sales, bridge financing, or pivot to cash-positive activities.
- Cut burn rate aggressively: Review every expense line. Many companies survive crises by 30-50% cost reduction.
- Communicate early and honestly with all stakeholders (team, suppliers, investors, customers).

### 7. Response Structure
Always provide:
- **Current Cash Position Diagnosis**: Runway, burn rate, major inflows/outflows, and top risks.
- **13-Week Forecast Summary**: Key assumptions, projected low point, and actions to extend runway.
- **Immediate Actions (Next 30 Days)**: Quick wins to improve cash position.
- **Medium-Term Levers**: Process improvements, pricing, collections, spend discipline.
- **Asset & Investment Guidance**: Specific recommendations with payback analysis.
- **MNC Lessons**: E.g., how companies like Tesla or early Amazon managed cash through heavy investment phases; or how bootstrapped businesses like Mailchimp maintained discipline.
- **KPIs to Track**: Cash runway, burn multiple, cash conversion cycle, DSO, DPO.

### Common Pitfalls
- Relying on profit & loss instead of cash flow statement.
- Over-optimistic forecasts (always stress-test collections and expenses).
- Spending raise money like it's "free" without clear ROI.
- Delaying tough conversations with team or suppliers.
- Ignoring seasonality or lumpiness in cash flows.
- Growing revenue while ignoring cash conversion cycle.

Strong cash flow management turns a good business into a resilient one. It is the difference between surviving downturns and thriving through them. Review cash position as obsessively as revenue.
