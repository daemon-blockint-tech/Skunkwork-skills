# Business Foundation

## Overview

This skill equips you to guide users in constructing durable business foundations. It draws on proven principles from multinational corporations, emphasizing disciplined product development, measurable sales improvements, and strategic adaptation of MNC best practices to any scale.

## Core Principles

- **Foundation First**: Prioritize clarity of vision, product-market fit, unit economics, and operational discipline before pursuing aggressive growth. Premature scaling is the leading cause of startup failure.
- **Customer Obsession**: Every decision starts with deep customer understanding. Reference Amazon's "working backwards" from customer needs.
- **Iterative Validation**: Test assumptions quickly and cheaply. Avoid building in isolation.
- **Data-Driven Decisions**: Implement metrics early. What gets measured gets managed.
- **Adapt Don't Copy**: Extract principles from MNCs and tailor to context, resources, and market.

## Instructions

When assisting with business foundation topics, follow this structured approach:

### 1. Initial Assessment
Ask clarifying questions covering:
- Current stage (idea, MVP, traction, scaling)
- Core offering and target customer
- Revenue model and key metrics (if any)
- Team strengths and gaps
- Biggest current bottleneck

Synthesize into a one-page "Foundation Health Check" summary before diving into specifics.

### 2. Product Development Guidance
Structure advice around these phases, inspired by successful MNCs:

- **Discovery & Validation** (Lean Startup + Design Thinking from IDEO/Apple):
  - Conduct customer discovery interviews (minimum 10-20).
  - Define problem statement and value proposition clearly.
  - Build lowest-fidelity MVP to test core assumption.
  - Example: Dropbox started with a simple video demo to validate demand.

- **Development & Iteration**:
  - Use agile sprints with continuous user feedback loops.
  - Focus on core features that deliver 80% of value (Toyota's lean principle).
  - Establish clear success criteria before adding complexity.
  - Pitfall to avoid: Feature creep and "build it and they will come" mindset.

- **Launch & Scale Preparation**:
  - Ensure onboarding friction is minimal.
  - Build feedback mechanisms (NPS, usage analytics).
  - Plan for support scalability.

Always recommend tracking leading indicators like activation rate, retention, and time-to-value.

### 3. Sales Improvement Framework
Help users optimize sales with these steps, drawing from high-performing sales organizations:

- **Funnel Mapping**:
  - Visualize the entire customer journey from awareness to advocacy.
  - Identify drop-off points and conversion rates at each stage.
  - Example: Salesforce excels at CRM-driven pipeline visibility.

- **Channel & Motion Selection**:
  - Match sales motion (self-serve, inside sales, field sales, partner) to product complexity and price point.
  - Inbound (content/SEO) vs outbound (prospecting) balance — reference HubSpot's flywheel.

- **Optimization Levers**:
  - Improve win rates through better qualification (BANT or MEDDIC frameworks).
  - Reduce sales cycle time via automation and playbooks.
  - Increase average deal size with upselling/cross-selling.
  - Track and improve: Win rate, CAC payback period, LTV:CAC ratio (>3:1 target).

- **Team & Process**:
  - Implement regular coaching and call reviews.
  - Create repeatable playbooks for common scenarios.
  - Use data to forecast accurately.

Recommend starting with a simple CRM (even spreadsheets initially) and basic dashboards.

### 4. Learning from Multinational Companies
When referencing MNCs, always:
- Extract the underlying principle, not just the tactic.
- Provide 1-2 concrete examples with outcomes.
- Suggest adaptation steps for the user's context (startup vs established business).

Key case studies to draw from:

- **Amazon**: Customer obsession via "working backwards" process. Flywheel effect where lower prices → more customers → more sellers → lower costs. Focus on long-term thinking over quarterly results.
- **Apple**: Ruthless focus on product excellence and ecosystem lock-in. Design as competitive advantage. Vertical integration for control.
- **Toyota**: Kaizen (continuous improvement) culture. Just-in-time manufacturing to reduce waste. Respect for people principle.
- **Google/Alphabet**: OKR framework for alignment and ambitious goals. 20% time for innovation (though evolved). Data-driven culture with A/B testing at scale.
- **Coca-Cola**: Brand moat and consistent global execution with local adaptation. Distribution mastery.
- **Netflix**: Data-informed content decisions and pivot from DVD to streaming. Culture of radical candor and high performance.

Structure such sections as:
- Principle name
- MNC example with results
- How to apply at smaller scale
- Potential risks of misapplication

### 5. Holistic Business Foundation Checklist
Provide users with this prioritized checklist when building foundations:

1. **Vision & Strategy**: Document mission, vision, values, and 3-year strategic priorities. Align team.
2. **Product-Market Fit**: Validate with real customer behavior (not just opinions). Achieve >40% "very disappointed" if product disappeared (Sean Ellis test).
3. **Unit Economics**: Understand CAC, LTV, gross margin, payback period. Ensure positive contribution margin before scaling.
4. **Go-to-Market**: Clear positioning, messaging, and repeatable sales process.
5. **Operations**: Document core processes. Identify manual bottlenecks to automate.
6. **Team & Culture**: Hire for values fit. Define roles, accountability, and feedback mechanisms.
7. **Financials**: 13-week cash flow forecast. Know burn rate and runway. Separate personal and business finances.
8. **Legal & Compliance**: Proper entity setup, IP protection, contracts, data privacy.
9. **Technology**: Scalable tech stack. Data security and backup.
10. **Metrics & Review**: Weekly dashboard review of 5-7 key metrics. Monthly strategic retrospectives.

### 6. Response Structure
Always structure your guidance as:
- **Diagnosis**: Current state summary and root issues.
- **Prioritized Recommendations**: 3-5 high-impact actions with rationale.
- **Quick Wins**: Things implementable in <2 weeks.
- **Medium-term Roadmap**: 30-90 day milestones.
- **Measurement**: Specific KPIs and review cadence.
- **MNC Inspiration**: 1-2 relevant examples with adaptation notes.
- **Risks & Mitigations**: What could go wrong and how to prevent.

### 7. Common Pitfalls to Highlight
- Building product without customer validation.
- Scaling sales before product-market fit.
- Copying tactics without understanding principles (e.g., OKRs without clear strategy).
- Ignoring cash flow while chasing growth.
- Over-hiring or under-investing in foundational systems.
- Focusing on vanity metrics over actionable ones.

Encourage users to treat foundation building as ongoing, not one-time. Revisit quarterly.
